Information for Dad: Your Right to Press Charges

If you change your mind and want to press criminal charges against Maia, you absolutely can. Even though you initially told the police officers that you did not want to press charges, that does not close the case forever. In Indiana, the ultimate decision to file formal criminal charges belongs to the county prosecutor, not to the victim or the police. Because the police already responded to the scene and witnessed the video evidence of her behavior, a formal record of the incident already exists.

The Legal Deadlines (Statute of Limitations)

Under Indiana law, you have a significant amount of time to change your statement and cooperate with the state.

For Domestic Battery and simple assault, which is a Class A misdemeanor, the state gives you up to two full years from the date of the incident to move forward with charges.

For Residential Entry, which involves charging at the door and forcefully breaking back into your home, the crime is classified as a Level 6 Felony. For this felony charge, the state has up to five full years to file formal charges against her.

Why Moving Forward Quickly is Safer for Your Home

Although the law gives you two to five years to act, changing your mind right now is the fastest way to secure your home. First, because the state has recently filed a formal probation violation against Maia, a fresh criminal accusation gives the prosecutor immediate leverage. The prosecutor can request that a judge immediately revoke her current bond and issue an arrest warrant. If the judge signs this warrant, the police will arrest her and hold her in jail long before her scheduled June court date. Second, moving forward now ensures that the responding officers' memories are fresh and that their body camera footage is easily preserved. Finally, having an active criminal case for domestic assault against her completely undermines any defense she has to stay in your house during a civil eviction.

Exactly How to Change Your Mind Tomorrow

You do not need to wait for the police to return to your house to fix this situation. You can take action by calling the Logansport Police Department non-emergency line at (574) 753-4101. Give them the date of the incident, and tell them that you were shaken up at the time but now wish to provide a formal statement to press charges for your own safety. Alternatively, you can walk directly into the Cass County Prosecutor's Office located inside the courthouse at 200 Court Park. Tell the clerk or a victim advocate that you are an elderly victim of domestic abuse and want to ensure the state moves forward with the assault and break-in charges.
